üß† Mind Tools (What Your Brain Does)

üÖ∞Ô∏è M1: Acronyms & Acrostics

Making memorable phrases from first letters. Like "Only Hippos Are Happy Over Apples" for Sin-Cos-Tan (SOHCAHTOA), or "Please Excuse My Dear Aunt Sally" for math order of operations (PEMDAS).

Beginner: Use existing acronyms like PEMDAS
Intermediate: Create your own memorable phrases
Advanced: Make nested acronyms for complex subjects

üîó M2: Association

Connect new info to what you know. Like remembering "Victoria" by thinking of Queen Victoria, or linking "mitochondria = powerhouse" to your phone's battery.

Beginner: Find obvious connections (name similarities)
Intermediate: Create personal, meaningful links
Advanced: Build association chains and networks

üì¶ M3: Chunking

Breaking long info into groups. Like remembering phone numbers as 555-123-4567 instead of 5551234567, or learning vocab in groups of 5 words instead of 20 at once.

Beginner: Break long numbers into 3-4 digit groups
Intermediate: Group related concepts together
Advanced: Create hierarchical chunking systems

üè∞ M4: Memory Palace

Use your bedroom layout to remember a shopping list: milk on your pillow, bread on your desk, eggs by your closet. Walk through mentally to recall everything!

Beginner: Use your bedroom for 5-10 items
Intermediate: Use your whole house for 20+ items
Advanced: Create multiple palaces for different subjects

üîÅ M5: Repeating Things

Repeat a 4-digit locker combo 5 times each morning. The key is timing - don't just mindlessly repeat, but space it out strategically.

Beginner: Say it out loud 5-10 times in a row
Intermediate: Use spaced repetition - review after 1 hour, 1 day, 1 week
Advanced: Add rhythm or movement while repeating

üé∂ M6: Songs & Rhymes

Turn the periodic table into a rap song, or make history dates rhyme with silly words. Your brain loves rhythm and melody!

Beginner: Use existing tunes like 'Twinkle Star' for new lyrics
Intermediate: Create original rhythms and melodies
Advanced: Compose multi-verse songs for complex topics

üìñ M7: Storytelling

Make the Revolutionary War into a story about angry teenagers vs strict parents. Turn chemistry reactions into epic battles between elements!

Beginner: Create simple cause-and-effect stories
Intermediate: Add characters and emotions
Advanced: Build epic multi-chapter narratives

üëÅÔ∏è M8: Visualization

Picture a giant elephant wearing your math teacher's glasses to remember formulas. The weirder and funnier the image, the better it sticks!

Beginner: Create simple, funny mental images
Intermediate: Add movement and interaction to images
Advanced: Build complex visual narratives
